# Excel-Zomato-Restaurant-Data-Analysis
This project conducts an in-depth analysis of Zomato restaurant data using Microsoft Excel to uncover key trends and insights within the food service industry. The analysis focuses on understanding restaurant performance, customer preferences, and market dynamics across various locations and cuisines.

Key Objectives:
To explore and clean a Zomato dataset, ensuring data accuracy and consistency.
To analyze restaurant ratings, average costs, and the availability of features like online delivery and table booking.
To identify popular cuisines and their distribution across different cities and countries.
To understand the relationship between restaurant features (e.g., location, cuisine, average cost) and customer ratings.
To visualize key findings using Excel's charting and dashboard capabilities to present actionable insights.

Methodology:
Data Acquisition and Cleaning: The Zomato dataset was imported into Excel, followed by data cleaning techniques to handle missing values, duplicates, and inconsistencies.
Data Analysis: Utilized various Excel functions (e.g., VLOOKUP, SUMIF, COUNTIF, AVERAGEIF) and features (e.g., PivotTables, Conditional Formatting) to perform exploratory data analysis.
Visualization: Created interactive charts and dashboards within Excel to visually represent findings related to restaurant distribution, ratings, cuisine popularity, and cost analysis.

Key Insights & Findings (Examples):
Identification of top-rated restaurants and localities with high concentrations of specific cuisines.
Analysis of the impact of online delivery and table booking options on customer ratings.
Trends in average cost of dining across different cuisines and cities.
Geographic distribution of Zomato's services and restaurant types.
Total Restaurants by Average Ratings.
Total Restaurants by Price Bucket.
Table Booking VS. Online Booking.
No. of restaurants per country.
KPI cards like: AVG. Ratings, Countries , Total Restaurants , Total Cusines , Votes.



Tools Used:
Microsoft Excel (including PivotTables, Charts, and various functions, filter, KPI cards)
